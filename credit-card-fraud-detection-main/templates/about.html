Credit Card Fraud Detection
About the project

The online transactions has brought about an alarming rise in credit card fraud, posing a considerable challenge to financial institutions and consumers alike. Conventional fraud detection methods often struggle to keep pace with the evolving sophistication of fraudulent activities. In response, machine learning has emerged as a potent ally in the fight against fraud, offering the ability to sift through vast volumes of transactional data to detect suspicious patterns in real-time.

The Credit Card Fraud Detection project commences with complex data preprocessing and exploration, encompassing tasks such as data cleaning, feature engineering, and visualization to unearth critical insights from the dataset. With help ofthe logistic regression technique, the project delves into model development, where historical credit card transaction data is utilized to train and fine-tune the logistic regression model. Through iterative experimentation and evaluation, the logistic regression model's performance is optimized. The project explores the integration of additional machine learning algorithms and ensemble methods to the logistic regression model. The ultimate objective is to deploy a scalable and reliable Credit Card Fraud Detection system to safeguard financial institutions and consumers against fraudulent activities in the digital realm.
